Privacy
Policy

Last updated: March 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how TG Media ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses and protects any personal information you provide when using this website (tgmedia.uk). We are committed to ensuring your privacy is protected in accordance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

The data controller responsible for your personal data is TG Media, based in Warwick, United Kingdom. You can contact us at hello@tgmedia.uk.

What Information We Collect

We may collect the following personal information:

  • Your name and email address when you submit our contact form
  • The content of any message you send us
  • Basic technical data such as your IP address, browser type and pages visited (only if analytics are enabled — currently they are not)

We do not collect sensitive personal data. We do not use third-party tracking or advertising cookies.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information you provide solely to:

  • Respond to your enquiry or project request
  • Communicate with you about a potential or ongoing project
  • Comply with any legal obligations

We will never sell, rent or share your personal data with third parties for marketing purposes.

Legal Basis for Processing

Under UK GDPR, our legal basis for processing your personal data is:

  • Legitimate interests — responding to your enquiry and managing our business relationship with you
  • Contractual necessity — where you become a client, processing your data is necessary to fulfil our contract with you
  • Legal obligation — where we are required to retain certain records by law

How We Store Your Data

Contact form submissions are processed via Formspree and stored securely. We retain enquiry data for up to 12 months, after which it is deleted unless an ongoing client relationship exists.

We do not transfer your data outside the UK or European Economic Area (EEA) except where services such as Formspree process data in line with UK GDPR-equivalent protections.

Your Rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Rectification — ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Erasure — request that we delete your personal data where there is no legitimate reason to continue processing it
  • Restriction — ask us to restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances
  • Portability — request your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Objection — object to processing based on legitimate interests

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at hello@tgmedia.uk. We will respond within 30 days.

Third-Party Services

This website uses the following third-party services which may process data on our behalf:

  • Google Fonts — used to load typefaces. Google may log requests from your browser. See Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Formspree — used to process contact form submissions. See Formspree's Privacy Policy.
  • GitHub Pages — used to host this website. See GitHub's Privacy Statement.

Cookies

This website uses only technically necessary processes to function. We do not use tracking, analytics or advertising cookies. For more information see our Cookie Policy.

Complaints

If you are unhappy with how we handle your personal data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK's data protection authority, the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO): ico.org.uk or by calling 0303 123 1113.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The date at the top of this page reflects the most recent revision. Continued use of this website after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
