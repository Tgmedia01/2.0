Overview

Project Overview

Bar Studios is a social-first content production studio working with some of the world's most recognisable brands — including Red Bull, major sports organisations and global consumer brands. Their output spans documentary, short-form content, campaign production and live event coverage.

The project required a website that could serve as both a portfolio and a new business tool — a platform that immediately communicates the quality and scale of their production capability to prospective brand and agency clients who are evaluating content partners at the highest level.

02

The Challenge

The Challenge

Content production studios are judged almost entirely on the quality of their output. A brand marketing director evaluating production partners will spend less than thirty seconds on a portfolio site before forming a view. In that window, the digital experience needs to communicate capability, taste and cultural relevance without ambiguity.

Bar Studios' existing presence did not reflect the calibre of their client roster or the production quality of their work. For a studio operating at the level of Red Bull and comparable global brands, the disconnect was a genuine commercial liability — the website was underselling the studio to the exact audience they most needed to impress.

"When your clients include Red Bull, your website needs to feel like it belongs in the same room. It wasn't. We changed that."

03

The Approach

The Approach

We began with one organising principle: the work leads. Every structural and visual decision was made in service of getting the user to Bar Studios' best content as quickly as possible, and creating an environment where that content could be experienced at the scale and quality it deserves.

The UX architecture was stripped of everything that didn't directly serve the portfolio. Navigation is minimal. The about and contact sections are present but subordinate. The homepage is almost entirely occupied by featured project content — large-format, immersive and immediate.

Work First

The homepage leads with featured projects at maximum scale. Context and credentials come after the work has already landed.

Editorial Layout

A magazine-style grid for the project archive — asymmetric, deliberately varied, reflecting the variety of production output.

Fast to Contact

New business enquiries are one step away from any point in the site. Friction between interest and contact has been removed entirely.

04

Design System

Design System

The visual language draws from editorial and fashion publishing — monochrome, bold, confident. A design system built to complement rather than compete with the visual content it presents.

Typography

Extended sans-serif for all display text — wide tracking, high contrast between heading weights and body copy. Headline typography is aggressive in scale and confident in weight. The type system references editorial publishing rather than digital convention.

Colour

Near-black primary with near-white secondary. Monochrome by design — colour enters the site exclusively through the project photography and video content. The neutral backdrop is deliberate: it gives high-production visual content the space to make its own impact.

Grid

An asymmetric editorial grid for the project archive — deliberately non-uniform to reflect the variety and dynamism of Bar Studios' output. Projects are presented at different scales within the grid, with larger placements reserved for flagship campaigns.

Motion

Video-first homepage. Featured projects autoplay on loop in the hero position — muted, cropped for impact. Hover states on the project grid reveal project title and category with a clean opacity transition. Nothing gratuitous.

05

Development

Development

Built on a custom CMS framework that gives the Bar Studios team full control over their project archive — adding new work, updating case studies and managing featured homepage content without requiring development resource. The system was designed with their workflow in mind: fast to update, structured enough to maintain visual consistency across every new addition.

Performance with video content was a primary technical challenge. The site makes extensive use of embedded and hosted video across the project pages and homepage. We implemented adaptive loading, fallback poster images and optimised delivery to maintain fast load times without compromising the immersive video experience that defines the brand.
