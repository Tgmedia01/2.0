Overview

Project Overview

Purple Granite is one of the UK's most established stone worktop specialists, operating showrooms in Kidderminster and Worcester and supplying granite, quartz, porcelain and marble worktops nationally. The company fabricates and installs entirely in-house — a direct operation that gives them a significant quality and speed advantage over the majority of the market.

The project required a complete digital overhaul — from brand expression through to e-commerce experience — to match the premium positioning of the product range and better serve the modern home buyer who expects to research and shortlist entirely online before committing to a showroom visit or purchase.

02

The Challenge

The Challenge

Stone worktops are a considered purchase. The decision-making process is inherently visual — customers are trying to imagine how a material will look in their specific kitchen, with their specific cabinetry, under their specific light conditions. This creates a fundamental tension in the buying journey: the thing the customer most needs to see is the one thing a conventional product catalogue cannot show them.

For Purple Granite, this meant that despite offering an exceptional product range and a best-in-class installation service, a significant proportion of potential customers were dropping off before reaching the enquiry stage — unable to bridge the gap between a flat product photograph and the finished result in their home.

"The gap between product catalogue and customer confidence was costing enquiries. We needed to close that gap with technology."

03

The Approach

The Approach

We approached this as a UX problem before a design problem. The primary friction point in the buying journey was the inability to visualise the product in context. That meant the solution had to be functional first — a tool that genuinely removes doubt — with the broader design system built around supporting it.

The website architecture was restructured around three core user intentions: browse the range, visualise the product, and make an enquiry. Every design decision — navigation structure, product page layout, CTA placement — was made in service of moving the user efficiently through those three stages.

UX First

Architecture mapped around the customer decision journey — browse, visualise, enquire.

Product Led

Stone photography at maximum scale — material texture and character visible at every stage.

Conversion Focus

Every page has a single, unambiguous next step. No dead ends in the buying journey.

04

Design System

Design System

The visual language was built to reflect the premium nature of the product — natural stone is inherently tactile, weighty and luxurious. The digital environment needed to carry that sense of material quality without resorting to visual excess.

Typography

A refined serif for product headings — referencing the craft and permanence of natural stone — paired with a clean sans-serif for UI and body copy. The typographic contrast creates a clear premium signal without feeling dated or decorative.

Colour

Near-white and warm stone tones as the primary palette — allowing the product photography to carry the colour story rather than competing with it. Purple as a precise brand accent, used sparingly for CTAs, active states and key interaction points.

Layout

Large-format product imagery at every opportunity. Stone textures deserve space. Product grids are generous — fewer products per row, each given room to communicate material quality. White space is as much a design element as the photography.

Photography Direction

Slab photography at close crop to show veining and texture. Kitchen lifestyle imagery selected for accuracy rather than aspiration — real environments, real light conditions, real installation quality.

05

Key Feature

The Worktop
Visualiser

Custom Interactive Tool

The centrepiece of the Purple Granite build is a bespoke interactive worktop visualiser — a custom-built tool that allows customers to preview different stone materials and finishes applied directly within a photorealistic kitchen environment.

Rather than asking customers to imagine how a product will look in their kitchen, the visualiser removes that cognitive burden entirely. Customers can switch between granite, quartz, marble and porcelain options — comparing materials, colours and vein patterns in real time — within a rendered kitchen scene that reflects realistic light and shadow conditions.

Material Library

Full product range accessible in the visualiser — granite, quartz, marble and porcelain. Materials render with accurate colour and vein mapping.

Kitchen Environment

Multiple kitchen scene options covering different cabinet colours, styles and layouts — so customers can match to their specific context.

Real-time Switching

Direct to Enquiry

From the visualiser, customers can move directly to request a sample, book a showroom visit or request a quote — all within one continuous journey.

06

Development

Development

The website is built on Shopify — selected for its robust product management capabilities, its native e-commerce infrastructure and its ability to handle the breadth of the Purple Granite range across multiple material categories. The custom theme was built entirely from scratch to the design specification, with no reliance on templated frameworks.

The visualiser is a bespoke JavaScript application integrated into the product pages — built to handle high-resolution material textures without performance compromise. Canvas-based rendering applies material maps to the kitchen environment in real time, with optimised asset loading to maintain speed across all devices including mobile. The tool was tested extensively across viewport sizes, connection speeds and device types before launch.
