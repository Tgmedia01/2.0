Overview

Project
Overview

Westbury Music is an independent music publisher operating at the intersection of creative talent and global commerce. Their sync licensing operation places tracks in film, television, advertising and gaming for brands from Nike and PlayStation to Netflix and the UEFA Champions League.

The project required a website serving two distinct audiences: artists seeking a publishing partner, and music supervisors seeking licensable content fast. One platform, no compromise.

02
Challenge

The
Challenge

"A publishing company of this standing needed a platform that matched the weight of its catalogue — structured, direct, built to be taken seriously."

The previous presence did not reflect the calibre of the catalogue. For a business whose currency is credibility, the disconnect between quality of work and quality of website was a tangible commercial problem. A music supervisor browsing for sync makes judgements in seconds.

Approach

The
Approach

01

Dual Audience Architecture

We began by mapping two user journeys in parallel — the artist and the music supervisor. Rather than two separate experiences, we identified their shared language: quality, credibility, clarity.

02

Content-First Structure

The Roster section presents each artist with context — not just a name but a catalogue reference that speaks to their value. The Sync section is a functional tool, not a showcase, built for speed.

03

Trust Signals

Brand placement logos — Nike, PlayStation, Netflix, UEFA — positioned to communicate the scale of the operation within the first scroll. The work earns the authority before the copy has to.

Design System

Design
System

The visual direction derived from the brand's existing identity — a teal and dark palette carrying both the warmth of a creative organisation and the authority of a commercial one.

Typography

Aa Bb Cc

Clean sans-serif hierarchy. Large, confident headings paired with structured body copy. Type calibrated to read at pace — designed for music supervisors who don't have time to browse.

Colour

Dark navy and near-black primary — communicates seriousness without severity. Teal as precise accent for interaction states. White space used generously to give the catalogue room to breathe.

Layout

A rigid grid system maintains consistency across every content type — artist profiles, sync listings, featured placements and brand logos all occupy defined structural positions. Grid is the discipline that holds the variety together.

Motion

Subtle. Purposeful. Animation is present only where it adds clarity — content transitions, hover states, featured project carousels. Nothing decorative. Motion is earned by function.

Development

Built to
Scale.

Built on WordPress with a custom theme — selected for its flexibility in handling varied content types and the team's ability to update catalogue listings, add sync placements and manage the roster independently after handover.

Performance was primary. Large catalogues with imagery and embedded media can be slow. We implemented lazy loading, optimised asset delivery and structured the database queries to keep load times fast regardless of catalogue size.

Scalable platform built to grow alongside the company's roster
