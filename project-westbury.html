Overview

Project Overview

Westbury Music is an independent music publisher operating at the intersection of creative talent and global commerce. Their catalogue spans decades of recorded music, and their sync licensing operation places tracks in film, television, advertising and gaming for some of the world's most recognised brands — from Nike and PlayStation to Netflix and the UEFA Champions League.

The project required a website that could simultaneously serve two distinct audiences: artists and labels looking for a trusted publishing partner, and music supervisors seeking licensable content fast. One platform needed to speak credibly to both without compromise.

02

The Challenge

The Challenge

The previous digital presence did not reflect the calibre of the company's catalogue or the sophistication of their client base. For a business whose currency is credibility — in both the creative and commercial music worlds — the disconnect between the quality of the work and the quality of the website was a tangible problem.

The challenge was to build something that felt authoritative without being cold, and creative without sacrificing structure. Music publishing is a niche industry where first impressions carry significant weight. A music supervisor browsing for sync opportunities makes judgements within seconds. The platform needed to earn trust immediately.

"A publishing company of this standing needed a platform that matched the weight of its catalogue — structured, direct, and built to be taken seriously."

03

The Approach

The Approach

We began by mapping the two user journeys in parallel — the artist seeking representation and the music supervisor seeking content. Rather than designing two separate experiences, we identified the shared language between them: quality, credibility, clarity.

The information architecture was rebuilt from the ground up. The Roster section was structured to present each artist with context — not just a name and headshot but a positioning statement and catalogue reference that speaks to their value as a publishing asset. The Sync section was designed as a functional tool rather than a showcase, allowing supervisors to move quickly from interest to contact.

04

Design System

Design System

The visual direction was derived from the brand's existing identity — a teal and dark palette that carries both the warmth of a creative organisation and the authority of a commercial one. We extended this into a structured digital language built around restraint and precision.

Typography

A clean sans-serif hierarchy — large, confident headings paired with structured body copy. Type sizes are deliberately calibrated so content reads at pace, not at leisure. The roster and sync sections use tabular-style layouts where typographic discipline does the structural work.

Colour

Dark background primary — deep navy and near-black — with the brand's teal used as a precise accent for interaction states and featured content. The palette communicates seriousness without severity. White space is used generously to give the catalogue room to breathe.

Layout

A rigid grid system maintains consistency across every content type — artist profiles, sync listings, featured placements and brand partner logos all occupy defined structural positions. The grid is the discipline that holds the variety of content together.

Motion

Subtle. Purposeful. Animation is present only where it adds clarity — content transitions, hover states, featured project carousels. Nothing is decorative. Motion is earned by function.

05

Development

Development

Built on WordPress with a custom theme — selected for its flexibility in handling varied content types and the Westbury team's ability to update catalogue listings, add new sync placements and manage the roster independently after handover.

Performance was a primary consideration. Large music catalogues with imagery and embedded media can be slow. We implemented lazy loading, optimised asset delivery and structured the database queries to keep load times fast regardless of catalogue size. The site is fully responsive — designed and tested across all viewports with the mobile experience treated as a primary context, not an afterthought.
